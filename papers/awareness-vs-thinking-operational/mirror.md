## Summary
This note separates awareness and thinking in a strictly operational sense. Awareness is the low-cost monitoring and gating layer that reads telemetry, selects posture, and enforces commit rights. Thinking is the bounded activation burst that produces structured candidate models, falsifiers, and intervention proposals.

The note does **not** introduce a theory of mind. It sharpens an engineering distinction already implicit in the Baseline corpus: remain near a cheap monitoring baseline, escalate evidence when uncertainty is high, enter bounded analysis only when warranted, and allow durable updates only under admissibility and phase discipline.
